Computer History Timeline

ENIAC

1945

Brief Description: The ENIAC was the first general-purpose electronic digital computer, using nearly 18,000 vacuum tubes to perform high-speed calculations. It replaced slower electromechanical machines.

Performance Impact: ENIAC performed thousands of calculations per second, orders of magnitude faster than humans or mechanical devices. Despite its bulky size and complex wiring, it demonstrated the practicality of electronic computing and set the stage for more advanced digital computers.
