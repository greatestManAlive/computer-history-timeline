Computer History Timeline

Hollerith’s Tabulating Machine

1890

Brief Description: Herman Hollerith’s tabulating machine used punched cards to automate data processing for the U.S. Census. It replaced manual counting with electromechanical tabulation.

Performance Impact: The machine reduced census processing time from 10 years to 3 years, proving the power of automation for large datasets. It marked the beginning of modern data processing and led to the founding of IBM, shaping business and government computing for decades.
